Before You Facilitate

Start at the home page — it explains the tool and helps you pick a planner, check-in, or dashboard. This guide focuses on the part the hub can’t do for you: leading the conversation well.

Keep the framing developmental, not evaluative. The aim is to surface patterns and choose one next step, never to rate individual teachers. Nothing is stored or transmitted; members decide whether to share a response code, and the dashboard shows team patterns rather than individual results.

Running the Process

Step Facilitator Move Approx. Time
1. Choose the check-in Decide whether the team needs the full Compass or one short check-in, and share the core question so everyone knows the focus. 2–3 min
2. Frame the purpose Explain that this is developmental, not evaluative. Emphasize voluntary sharing. 3–5 min
3. Reflect individually Members complete the check-in privately and think of evidence before rating. 3–15 min
4. Generate the report Paste voluntarily shared codes from the same check-in into the dashboard. 3–5 min
5. Discuss evidence Start with evidence before solutions: “What examples might explain this pattern?” 10–15 min
6. Choose one next step Complete the action plan: one readiness condition to strengthen before the next cycle. 5–10 min

The check-in and dashboard both export through your browser’s print dialog — choose “Save as PDF.”

Reading the Scores

Ratings are evidence-informed conversation starters, not measurement scores. Treat a low number as a signal to discuss, not a verdict, and ask for evidence — agendas, student work, decisions, follow-up, moments of trust — before interpreting it.

Average Score Readiness Level Recommended Response
1.0–1.9 Foundational Concern Begin with structures, trust, norms, and clarity before expecting deeper PLC work.
2.0–2.79 Emerging Identify one condition to strengthen and create a short-term action step.
2.8–3.49 Developing Use existing strengths to deepen inquiry, application, and follow-up.
3.5–4.0 Established Move toward more advanced practices such as shared inquiry cycles, peer feedback, or instructional artifacts.

Facilitation Moves

  • Discuss team conditions, not individual performance.
  • Ask for evidence before interpreting scores.
  • Treat disagreement as useful data.
  • Choose one next step rather than trying to fix every area at once.
  • Revisit the action plan at a future PLC meeting.

Framework Reference

This tool is grounded in Hord and Sommers’ five components of professional learning communities: shared and supportive leadership, shared values and vision, collective learning and application, shared personal practice, and supportive conditions (Hord & Sommers, 2008).

In the menu, the component Hord and Sommers call Shared Values and Vision appears as Shared Purpose for clarity with practitioners; the framework name is retained here so the connection to the original five components stays clear.

Hord, S. M., & Sommers, W. A. (2008). Leading professional learning communities: Voices from research and practice. Corwin Press.
