What the PLC Readiness Compass is

The PLC Readiness Compass is a flexible reflection and facilitation hub that helps professional learning communities notice readiness conditions, guide honest conversation, and choose one meaningful next step.

What this is not: not a teacher evaluation tool, not a compliance checklist, and not a validated psychometric instrument. Scores are developmental conversation starters, not measurement claims. Nothing here is stored or transmitted; responses stay in your browser unless someone chooses to share a response code.

Five Readiness Areas

These five conditions are the map the whole Compass is built around. A short check-in looks at one; the full Compass looks at all five.

Shared Leadership

Teacher voice, shared ownership, and protected time, even when a lead or administrator guides the agenda.

Shared Purpose

A shared reason for meeting that connects to student learning, not just department operations.

Collective Learning

Conversations that use evidence, try ideas, and follow up on whether they helped students.

Shared Practice

Making real teaching practice visible to one another in low-risk, supportive ways.

Supportive Conditions

The time, trust, norms, and access that let meaningful PLC work actually happen.

“Shared Purpose” is the user-facing label for the framework component Hord and Sommers call “Shared Values and Vision.” The full framework language is preserved in the Facilitator Guide.

How the Process Works

Reflection comes first; the dashboard is step three, not the starting point.

1

Reflect privately

Each member completes a check-in on their own, thinking of evidence before choosing a rating.

2

Share voluntarily

Members choose whether to share a response code. Written evidence notes always stay private.

3

Generate a team report

The dashboard turns shared codes into a conversation report that surfaces patterns and perception gaps.

4

Choose one next step

The team names one realistic move to strengthen before the next PLC cycle, rather than fixing everything at once.

Read the Guide
Not sure whether to use the Full Compass or a Short Check-In?

Use the Full Compass when…

  • You are starting or restarting PLC work.
  • It is the beginning of the year or midyear.
  • The team needs a broad readiness baseline.
  • You want to look across all five readiness areas.

Use a Short Check-In when…

  • The team only has 10–15 minutes.
  • One readiness condition needs attention.
  • You want to reduce fatigue.
  • The team is in the middle of an ongoing PLC cycle.

Both live in the Check-In Menu — the full Compass is built in as one of the options, so there is no separate version to open.
